Designing a Digital Banking Experience Built on Trust

Reducing friction in onboarding and improving clarity in high-risk financial flows

+18% Onboarding completion
-25% Drop-off in verification
-30% Support requests

Context

The product is a digital banking application designed for everyday financial operations: onboarding, identity verification, account management, and money transfers.

My role was end-to-end product design, working closely with product managers, engineers, and compliance teams.

Problem

We observed significant drop-offs during onboarding and early product usage.

  • Users abandoned identity verification steps
  • Low completion rate of onboarding flow
  • Hesitation when performing financial actions

The core issue was not usability alone — it was lack of trust in high-risk financial interactions.

Insight

Users don’t treat banking apps as software — they treat them as financial systems where mistakes feel irreversible.

Every unclear step increases perceived risk, especially when money or identity is involved.

Hypothesis

If we reduce cognitive load and increase transparency in critical flows, we can improve onboarding completion and increase user trust.

Solution

Simplified Onboarding

Reduced unnecessary steps and improved clarity in identity verification flow.

Transparent Transfers

Redesigned money transfer flow to clearly show what happens at each step.

Clear Account Overview

Simplified financial data presentation to improve at-a-glance understanding.

Key Decision

We intentionally decided not to remove security steps, even though it could have improved speed.

Instead, we redesigned them to feel more transparent and predictable, ensuring users always understood what was happening with their data and money.

Impact

  • +18% onboarding completion
  • -25% verification drop-off
  • -30% support tickets

The redesign improved both usability and perceived trust in the product, especially in high-risk financial interactions.

What I Learned

In fintech, clarity and trust are more important than speed.

Users are willing to spend more time if they feel in control and understand the system.

Next Step

  • Explore adaptive onboarding based on user behaviour
  • Improve personalization of financial dashboards
  • Introduce predictive guidance for transfers
